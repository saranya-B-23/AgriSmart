🌾 Rice

  • Soil: Clayey, water-retentive
  • Watering: Requires standing water
  • Fertilizer: High Nitrogen (Urea)
  • Pest Control: Stem borer, blast (use neem spray)
  • Season: June–July

🌽 Maize

  • Soil: Loamy, rich in humus
  • Watering: Medium, avoid waterlogging
  • Fertilizer: NPK blend
  • Pest Control: Stem borer, aphids
  • Season: Kharif and Rabi

☕ Coffee

  • Soil: Red soil, humus-rich
  • Watering: Requires regular rainfall or drip
  • Fertilizer: Compost + potassium
  • Pest Control: Coffee berry borer
  • Season: November–March

🍌 Banana

  • Soil: Fertile loam, well-drained
  • Watering: Needs heavy irrigation
  • Fertilizer: Compost + Urea monthly
  • Pest Control: Aphids, banana beetle
  • Season: All year

🧺 Cotton

  • Soil: Black or loamy
  • Watering: Weekly, no overwatering
  • Fertilizer: Potash-based
  • Pest Control: Bollworm, whitefly
  • Season: June–September

🍇 Grapes

  • Soil: Well-drained sandy loam
  • Watering: Moderate, avoid humidity
  • Fertilizer: Nitrogen + micronutrients
  • Pest Control: Powdery mildew, use sulfur sprays
  • Season: December–February

🍎 Apple

  • Soil: Loamy, well-aerated
  • Watering: Weekly, more during fruiting
  • Fertilizer: FYM + DAP
  • Pest Control: Codling moth
  • Season: November–March (hill regions)

🥬 Mungbean

  • Soil: Sandy loam or black soil
  • Watering: Less frequent, drought-tolerant
  • Fertilizer: Light urea dose + organic manure
  • Pest Control: Leaf hoppers, whitefly
  • Season: Summer and Kharif

🍅 Tomato

  • Soil: Loamy, rich in organic matter
  • Watering: Regular but not waterlogged
  • Fertilizer: NPK + potash spray
  • Pest Control: Fruit borer, aphids
  • Season: All year except monsoon

🌰 Chickpea

  • Soil: Well-drained sandy loam
  • Watering: Minimal, drought-resistant
  • Fertilizer: Super phosphate + FYM
  • Pest Control: Pod borer, wilt
  • Season: Rabi (Oct–Nov)
📌 Note: These are general tips. For accurate region-specific advice, consult your local Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or agricultural extension officer.
